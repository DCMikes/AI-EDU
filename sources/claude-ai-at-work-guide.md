## Introduction: Unlocking the Power of AI Collaboration

With the introduction of Claude Opus 4.6, Anthropic's most intelligent model to date,
AI collaboration has reached new heights. Released February 5, 2026, Opus 4.6 is
purpose-built for agentic work, deep reasoning, and professional knowledge tasks. It
introduces **adaptive thinking** — a dynamic reasoning system that automatically
calibrates how much to think based on the complexity of each request.

budget, Opus 4.6 dynamically determines how much reasoning each task requires — from a

## Part 1: Getting Started with Claude AI

### Understanding Claude as Your Virtual Collaborator

Claude functions as a dynamic AI assistant that grows and adapts with your workflow.

How this sets Claude apart:
- Maintains context across conversations, eliminating the need to repeat information
- Learns your preferences over time for more targeted, relevant responses
- Adapts to your communication style for more natural collaboration
- Scales seamlessly from simple tasks to complex projects

**Adaptive thinking** (replaces manual extended thinking from 3.7 Sonnet):
- **Adaptive by default:** Claude picks up contextual cues to determine reasoning needed
- **Effort controls:** four levels — low, medium, high (default), and max — via the
  `/effort` parameter
- **Max effort:** a new level exclusive to Opus 4.6, unlocking peak reasoning depth
- **No budget-setting required:** manual reasoning budgets are replaced by adaptive
  reasoning

### Quick Setup: Your First Claude Project in 10 Minutes

Projects let you create dedicated AI assistants that maintain context across multiple
interactions. (Content below was hidden behind a Step One–Five tab widget on the page.)

- **Step One:** Open Claude AI and navigate to "Projects"
- **Step Two — Define your project scope:**
  - Primary objective (e.g., content creation, data analysis)
  - Key workflows to optimize
  - Desired outputs and formats
  - Success metrics
- **Step Three — Upload relevant documents:**
  - Past reports and analyses
  - Style guides and brand documentation
  - Process documentation
  - Historical data and metrics
- **Step Four — Set interaction guidelines:**
  - Preferred output formats
  - Required level of detail
  - Specific terminology or frameworks
  - Review and approval processes
- **Step Five — Start with a test task:**
  - Choose a simple, well-defined task
  - Evaluate Claude's output
  - Refine your prompts based on results
  - Document what works best

### AI Executive Assistant

With Opus 4.6's agentic capabilities, your executive assistant can manage longer-horizon
tasks autonomously — coordinating across multiple tools, drafting follow-ups, and
escalating only when a decision is needed.

Setup: create a dedicated Projects workspace; upload meeting transcripts, team

briefing review, priority task identification, communication drafting, follow-up
management. Upload meeting transcripts and Claude extracts decisions, action items, and

## Part 4: Advanced Implementation & Best Practices

### Scaling Across Teams

Take a phased approach — pilot teams demonstrating quick wins before broader deployment;
balance quick adoption with proper governance.

Essential components:
- **Infrastructure Setup:** security protocols, access controls, data management
  systems, integration frameworks
- **Team Development:** training programs, best practice documentation, support systems,
  success metrics

### Measuring Impact and ROI

Establish clear baselines before implementation; use regular check-ins and feedback.

Key performance indicators:
- **Efficiency Metrics:** time saved per task, error reduction rates, process
  improvement, resource optimization
- **Quality Improvements:** output accuracy, consistency rates, customer satisfaction,
  team productivity
