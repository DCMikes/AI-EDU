Accessing the brand-dcn Skill and Plugin

Written July 2, 2026 v1.2 Covers brand-dcn plugin 1.0.0

brand-dcn puts our brand voice and the full marketing asset library inside Claude, and it is already installed for everyone; IT publishes it to the whole team automatically. This page is about putting it to work: anything you write for doctors and their teams comes out sounding like one company, with the right colors, logos, fonts, and boilerplate already in hand.

Why

Why you would use this

Before this skill, staying on brand meant hunting through SharePoint for the current logo, the approved boilerplate, or the right template, and hoping the copy you found was the latest version. brand-dcn bundles a snapshot of the entire Brand Assets library, so Claude already knows our voice, our tagline, our palette, our logo rules, and our approved numbers when you sit down to write.

That matters for two reasons. First, consistency: a doctor who reads our emails, our sell sheets, and our social posts should hear the same humble, kind, confident voice everywhere. Second, speed: you spend your time on what the piece needs to say, and Claude handles how we say it. It is also a gentle on-ramp for newer team members, who can write like DCN from their first week.

How

How you set it up and use it

There is no setup

brand-dcn is published to the whole team through our internal plugin catalog and installs on its own, so you should already have it. If you want to confirm, just ask Claude "do you have the brand-dcn skill?" In the rare case it is missing, contact IT and we will sort out your access.

Calling it with /brand-dcn

The most direct way to leverage the skill is to lead your request with the slash command. Typing /brand-dcn loads the full brand context (voice rules, colors, logos, boilerplate, templates) before Claude starts writing, so the first draft lands on-brand instead of needing a rewrite. A few patterns worth stealing:

  1. Draft something new: "/brand-dcn write a follow-up email to Dr. Miller about her first digital case" or "/brand-dcn draft a social post promoting the August CE event."
  2. Fix something existing: paste or attach a draft and ask "/brand-dcn review this and bring it on-brand." Claude will flag and fix off-voice wording, banned phrases, wrong logos, and off-palette colors, and summarize what it changed.
  3. Ask brand questions: "/brand-dcn which logo goes on a dark slide?" or "/brand-dcn what are our exact hex colors?" It answers from the bundled guidelines rather than memory.
  4. Build from templates: "/brand-dcn start a one-pager from our letterhead about our implant workflow." The skill carries the PowerPoint and letterhead templates and the fonts to go with them.

It also triggers on its own

You do not have to remember the command. When you ask Claude to write or review anything doctor facing or customer facing, the skill activates automatically, and plain language works too: "make this on-brand" or "rewrite this in our voice." The slash command is simply the surest way to point Claude at the brand from the very first word.

Staying current

Marketing owns the master files and ships updates as new plugin versions, which reach you automatically through the catalog. If something in the skill looks out of date, tell Marketing so they can publish a refresh; please do not swap in files from other locations yourself, since the bundled snapshot is what keeps the whole team consistent.

Prompts

Three prompts to try

  1. "/brand-dcn write a welcome email to a practice that just sent us their first digital case."
  2. "/brand-dcn review the attached one-pager and bring it on-brand; tell me what you changed."
  3. "/brand-dcn I am building a dark-background slide; which logo file and hex colors should I use?"

One boundary worth knowing: the skill is for work that doctors, their teams, or the public will see. Purely internal writing does not need the brand lens, and the current month's content calendar should come from Marketing directly, since the bundled copy is a format example rather than the live calendar.
