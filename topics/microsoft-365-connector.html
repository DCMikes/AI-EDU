Configuring and Using the Microsoft 365 Connector

Written July 2, 2026 v1.1

The Microsoft 365 connector lets Claude search your Outlook mail and calendar, SharePoint, OneDrive, and Teams chats. Instead of copying and pasting content into the chat, you can ask Claude to go find the email, file, or conversation itself. It is built on MCP (Model Context Protocol), the standard Claude uses to talk to outside tools.

Why

Why you would use this

Most of what we know as a company lives in Microsoft 365: email threads with doctors, working documents on SharePoint, files on OneDrive, and day-to-day conversation in Teams. Without the connector, getting that context into Claude means hunting it down and pasting it in by hand. With the connector, Claude searches for it the same way you would, then works with what it finds.

Two properties make this safe to lean on. Access is read-only: Claude can search and read, but it cannot send email, edit or delete files, or change anything in Teams or SharePoint. And it mirrors your own permissions: Claude can only see what your Microsoft account can already see, nothing more.

How

How you set it up and use it

One-time setup (about two minutes)

IT has already done the organization-level work (adding the connector for our tenant and granting admin consent), so you only need to connect your own account:

  1. In Claude, open Settings, then Connectors.
  2. Find Microsoft 365 and click Connect.
  3. Sign in with your work account (your @dentalcrafters.net address). Personal Microsoft accounts will not work here.
  4. Approve the access prompt. That is it; the connector stays linked until you disconnect it.

Using it day to day

  1. Ask in plain language and name the source when you know it: "find the email thread with Dr. Smith about the bridge case," "search the Marketing SharePoint for the trade show checklist," "what is on my calendar Thursday," or "find a meeting time that works for me and Sarah next week."
  2. The first time Claude uses a given tool (say, Teams chat search) it may ask your approval. Approving once is normal; it is the connector working as designed.
  3. Combine it with other skills: for example, ask Claude to find last month's event recap email and turn it into an on-brand social post.

If something is not working

If Microsoft 365 does not appear in your Connectors list, the sign-in loops, or a search that should work comes back empty, contact IT and we will straighten out your access.

Prompts

Three prompts to try

  1. "Find the email thread with Dr. Hansen about the implant case, summarize where things stand, and draft a reply for my review."
  2. "Search the Marketing SharePoint for the trade show checklist and pull out the deadlines I need to hit this month."
  3. "Look at my calendar and Sarah's for next week and suggest three times that work for a 30 minute case review."

A few boundaries worth knowing as you go. The connector is read-only, so sending the email or editing the file is still your job; Claude drafts, you send. Brand assets are the one exception to "just search SharePoint": logos, templates, and boilerplate should come from the brand-dcn skill (see Accessing the brand-dcn Skill and Plugin) so everyone stays on the same released version. And as always, keep patient-identifiable information out of prompts and follow our data handling policies.
